## IMPORTANT DISCLAIMERS

1. This document is research, not legal advice. A licensed attorney must review all state determinations and tout-service assessments before launch.
2. State sports betting laws change frequently. This matrix requires quarterly review (see launch checklist).
3. Diamond Edge is an information/analysis service, NOT a sportsbook. We do not place bets or hold funds. This limits our regulatory exposure significantly, but does not eliminate it — especially regarding "tout service" and "information service" statutes, which vary by state.
4. "ALLOW" does not mean zero legal review. It means both operators are operational and no obvious blocking statute was identified for tout services. Attorney must clear each ALLOW state before launch.

## Tout Service / Handicapper Notes

Research did not identify states with explicit statutory registration requirements for sports-pick subscription services among the ALLOW states listed above. However:

1. **No state has been positively cleared of tout-service requirements without attorney review.** The "Attorney check" flag on every ALLOW state means attorney must confirm before launch.
2. **Louisiana** has historically had complex gambling regulations given its parish-based system. Attorney review is especially important here.
3. **New York** has aggressive consumer protection laws. Attorney should specifically review whether paid subscription pick services require any disclosure or registration.
4. **Tennessee** regulates operators strictly but no handicapper-specific requirement was identified for information-only services.
5. The distinction between "information service" and "tout service requiring licensure" varies by jurisdiction and is attorney-review territory.
